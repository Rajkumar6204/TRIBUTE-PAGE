NETAJI SUBHAS CHANDRA BOSE

"Give me blood, and I will give you freedom." - Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

Admirable Qualities

  • He was brave and a great freedom fighter. I like his way of revolutionary way of fighting. He was not confined to peaceful struggle which conceded nothing to Indians.
  • He was a great diplomat. He made the enemy's enemy his friend and consolidated his position. e.g He took the help of Japan to organize INA.
  • He was patriot of patriots. He gave the slogan "Give me blood, I will give you freedom" and thus aroused the patriotic feelings among masses.
  • He was a brilliant student and intellectual and cleared the Civil Service exam in his first attempt. I think without education, he could not have achieved such a milestone in India's struggle for freedom.
  • He believed Bhagwat Gita as a source of Inspiration. Good reading always shapes a man's behavior towards good.
  • He was among the first nationalists who demanded complete independence instead of Dominion status.

His Inspiring Story

    Subhas Chandra Bose, born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, was a prominent leader in India's struggle for independence against British colonial rule. His remarkable journey can be summarized as follows:

  • Early Years and Education: Bose showed early signs of brilliance and patriotism. He studied in Calcutta (now Kolkata) and was deeply influenced by Swami Vivekananda's teachings.
  • Leadership in Congress: Bose joined the Indian National Congress and rose to prominence with his emphasis on mass mobilization and direct action.
  • Dissatisfaction and Escape: Bose became disillusioned with non-violence and escaped house arrest to seek support from Axis powers against the British.
  • Formation of INA: In 1942, Bose established the Indian National Army (INA) in Japan, rallying Indian soldiers to fight alongside Japanese forces.
  • "Jai Hind" and Azad Hind Radio: Bose's leadership inspired soldiers with the slogan "Jai Hind," and he used Azad Hind Radio to galvanize Indians against British rule.
  • Azad Hind Government and Gender Equality: In 1943, Bose formed the Azad Hind Government in Singapore, and he championed gender equality by creating the Rani of Jhansi Regiment, a women's unit of the INA.
  • Legacy and Disappearance: Bose's plane crashed in Taiwan in 1945, but his legacy continued to inspire India's freedom movement and post-independence ideals.

    • Subhas Chandra Bose's life embodies courage, sacrifice, and determination, making him a timeless symbol of India's struggle for freedom and a source of inspiration for generations.
